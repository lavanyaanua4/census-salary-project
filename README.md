# Census Salary Analysis Project

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes the U.S. Census Income dataset to identify factors that influence whether an individual earns more than $50K annually. The analysis demonstrates real-world data analytics skills including data cleaning, exploratory data analysis, and insight generation.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Understand demographic and employment patterns affecting income
- Identify key factors associated with high earnings
- Analyze relationships between education, occupation, and salary
- Perform exploratory data analysis (EDA)

---

## 📂 Dataset Information
The dataset contains demographic and employment attributes such as:

- Age
- Education level
- Occupation
- Marital status
- Work hours per week
- Gender
- Native country
- Income category (>50K or <=50K)

## 📊 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

### 👩‍🎓 Education vs Income
Higher education levels strongly correlate with higher salary brackets.

### 💼 Occupation Impact
Certain professions show significantly higher earning potential.

### 💍 Marital Status Influence
Married individuals show higher probability of earning above $50K.

### ⏱ Working Hours Analysis
People working more hours per week tend to earn higher incomes.

### 🌍 Demographic Trends
Income distribution varies across age groups and demographics.

---

## 📈 Data Visualization
Created visual insights including:

- Income distribution charts
- Education vs income plots
- Occupation comparison graphs
- Age group analysis
- Correlation visualizations

---

## 💡 Key Insights
- Education level is one of the strongest predictors of income
- Professional occupations yield higher salaries
- Longer working hours are associated with higher earnings
- Married individuals show higher income probability
- Age plays a significant role in income distribution
